About Hayat Foundation

Why this project exists

Hayat Foundation is a community-led platform built to address a simple but devastating problem in Pakistan: every year, thousands of people die or suffer because they could not find a blood donor in time. We are trying to make that gap smaller, one connection at a time.

The problem in numbers

Pakistan needs more than 3 million units of blood every year. Despite this enormous need, voluntary donation rates remain stubbornly low — much of the country's supply still comes from paid donors or family replacement, both of which carry safety risks and create stressful situations for patient families.

The picture is most painful for thalassemia patients. Pakistan has one of the highest rates of beta-thalassemia in the world, with over 100,000 patients — most of them children — who require monthly blood transfusions to stay alive. Each missed transfusion shortens their life.

Add to that road accident victims, surgical patients, mothers suffering postpartum hemorrhage, and chemotherapy patients, and the daily demand on our blood banks is enormous.

One donation can save up to three lives. Blood is separated into red cells, plasma, and platelets — each component goes to a different patient in need.

What Hayat Foundation does

Hayat Foundation is a digital bridge between voluntary donors and patients. We don't store blood, we don't replace blood banks. Instead, we make finding the right donor faster and more transparent:

  • Donors register their blood group, city, area, and availability — and decide when they're ready to help.
  • Patients (or their attendants) submit verified requests with a doctor's recommendation, and immediately see matching donors nearby.
  • Admin verification protects both sides — only verified donors are shown publicly, and only requests with valid medical documentation are activated.
  • Direct contact via call or WhatsApp means no middleman, no commission, no delays.

Myths vs Facts

Myth: "Donating makes you weak"Your body replaces the donated blood within 24–48 hours, and red cells within 4–6 weeks. You can resume normal activity the same day.
Fact: Regular donors are healthierEach donation includes a free mini-health check (blood pressure, hemoglobin, infectious disease screening), helping you catch issues early.
Myth: "You can catch diseases by donating"Every donation uses brand-new, sterile, single-use equipment. There is zero risk of infection to the donor.
Fact: Donation takes just 10 minutesThe actual blood draw is 8–10 minutes. With registration and rest, the entire visit is under an hour.
Myth: "Only certain blood groups are needed"All blood groups are needed constantly. O−, however, is the universal donor and always in short supply.
Fact: One donation = up to 3 lives savedWhole blood is separated into red cells, plasma, and platelets — each helping a different patient.

Who can donate?

  • Age 18 to 65 years
  • Weight at least 50 kg
  • Hemoglobin ≥ 12.5 g/dL
  • No fever, infections, or current illness
  • No tattoos, piercings, or surgery in the past 6 months
  • Not pregnant or recently delivered (wait 9 months)
  • At least 3 months since your last donation

About this project

Hayat Foundation was built as a Civics and Community Engagement class project. The goal was not just to demonstrate a technical skill, but to apply civic values — service, responsibility, and active citizenship — to a real problem in our community.

Voluntary blood donation is one of the purest forms of citizenship: an anonymous gift, given freely, to a stranger you will never meet, that may save their life. We hope this small project inspires more people to make that gift when they turn 18.

"The blood you give is the life you give." Every drop matters. Every donor counts. Every life is worth saving.
